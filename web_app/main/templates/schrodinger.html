The Schrodinger Equation in the Diffusion Picture

The Schrodinger equation describes the evolution of quantum systems in time. One interesting question to ask if whether the Schrodinger equation has a diffusive nature to it.

More precisely, what information about the behaviour of the Schrodinger equation can we get if we compare it to the diffusion equation? \[ i \hbar \frac{d}{d t}|\Psi(t)\rangle=\hat{H}|\Psi(t)\rangle \]

The free particle potential

In the case analogous to a “free-moving particle”, the inductance of an LC circuit is much greater than its inductance. The Schrodinger equation looks like this: \[ i \hbar \frac{d}{d t}|\Psi(t)\rangle=\hat{H}|\Psi(t)\rangle \] \[ i \hbar \frac{d \Psi}{d t} = -\frac{\hbar^2}{2C} \frac{d^2 \Psi}{d \Phi^2} \] \[ \frac{d \Psi}{d t} = \frac{i\hbar}{2C} \frac{d^2 \Psi}{d \Phi^2} \]

Comparing the equations

We could suggest that the coefficient \( \frac{i\hbar}{2C} \) in the Schrodinger equation is an analogue of \( D \), the diffusion coefficient for the diffusion equation. Also, notice that in both cases we relate a single derivative in time \( \frac{d}{dt} \) is related to a double derivative in the space coordinate \( \frac{d^2 }{d \Phi^2} \)

\[ \frac{d \Psi}{d t} = \frac{i\hbar}{2C} \frac{d^2 \Psi}{d \Phi^2} \] \[ \frac{dT}{dt} = D \frac{d^2 T}{d x^2} \]

So, should we expect the same behaviour?

Diffusion behaviour

The evolution of the particles is different depending on the distribution curvature in the diffusion picture.

  • If the distribution is narrow, the curvature near the centre is large, then the particles will move very fast.
  • If the distribution is wide, the curvature near the centre is small, then the particles will move very slow.
We can conclude that the evolution of the distribution function is related to its curvature \( \frac{d^2 T}{dx^2} \), which follows from the statement of diffusion equation.

\[ \frac{dT}{dt} = D \frac{d^2 T}{dx^2} \]

Well, does the Schrodinger equation do the same?
